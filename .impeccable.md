## Design Context

### Users

Air Equivalent is for members of the public checking local outdoor-air conditions before making everyday decisions: going outside, exercising, commuting, caring for children or older relatives, or responding to smoke and pollution events. People may arrive for a quick glance on a phone or spend longer comparing monitors on a desktop. Their core jobs are to find a nearby monitor, understand how serious and how recent its PM2.5 reading is, translate an abstract concentration into a memorable but responsibly framed analogy, and verify the source and assumptions.

The experience must support touch, mouse, and keyboard input; common phone, tablet, and desktop layouts; slower mobile connections; color-vision differences; reduced motion; and screen-reader access through a synchronized non-map list.

### Brand Personality

**Observant, grounded, candid.** The interface should feel calm enough to support clear judgment, but capable of visible urgency when the data warrants it. Its voice is plainspoken and scientifically careful: never alarmist, jokey, clinical, or falsely precise. It distinguishes measured data from derived analogies and foregrounds timestamps, preliminary-data status, gaps, and uncertainty.

### Aesthetic Direction

A light, editorial environmental field journal: tactile paper-toned surfaces, dark botanical ink, fine rules, compact cartographic annotations, and an asymmetric map-first composition. Use a characterful editorial serif for display language and a humanist sans-serif for controls and body copy, sourced locally from dependable system stacks to avoid network font dependencies. The official AQI colors appear only where they carry meaning; every color signal is paired with text or shape.

The memorable gesture is a cartographer's field-note strip that connects the selected monitor, its true observation time, and the cigarette-equivalent assumption without turning the product into a generic analytics dashboard. Favor precise square or lightly softened geometry, hairline dividers, tabular numerals, and restrained motion over floating glass cards, loud gradients, dark cyber styling, pill-heavy controls, gamified smoking imagery, or decorative chart clutter.

### Design Principles

1. **Map first, evidence always.** Keep geography dominant while making source, observation time, freshness, and preliminary status easy to inspect.
2. **Make the analogy memorable, not literal.** Always attach the 24-hour persistence assumption and a path to the methodology; never imply that a person actually smoked cigarettes.
3. **Urgency follows data.** Let official AQI categories create emphasis, but keep the surrounding interface calm and never use color alone.
4. **Adapt the task, not just the size.** Use a persistent research rail on desktop, a touch-friendly bottom sheet on mobile, and an equivalent keyboard/screen-reader list at every size.
5. **Show uncertainty instead of sanding it away.** Missing concentration is “not available,” stale data stays visibly stale, sparse coverage never looks like clean air, and gaps remain gaps.
